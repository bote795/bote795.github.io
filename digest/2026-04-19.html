Agent Infrastructure Is Solidifying

The agent protocol layer is locking in. Three major platforms now support MCP natively, which means builders can write tools once and have them work across vendors — reducing switching costs and making agent infrastructure portable.

OpenAI's updated Agents SDK adds sandbox-aware orchestration with configurable memory. Agents can inspect files, run commands, and edit code inside controlled sandboxes. Includes Codex-style filesystem tools and standardized MCP integrations. If you're building coding agents, this is the toolkit to evaluate.

Microsoft Agent Framework 1.0 hit stable release with long-term support and full MCP v2.1 compatibility. It joins Claude Desktop and Cursor in supporting MCP natively. Three major platforms now speak the same agent protocol. MCP is becoming the USB-C of agent tooling.

Hermes Agent crossed 100K GitHub stars, making it the most-starred open-source agent framework — a credible alternative to commercial orchestration platforms. HermesOS also published its public roadmap, signaling a push toward a full open-source agent operating system with persistent memory and skill authoring.

Half of Product Managers Are Becoming Dinosaurs

Lenny's Podcast | Guest: Nikuel Singal (ex-Meta/Google, CPO Credit Karma) Watch here

Singal draws a hard line: ~50% of PMs built careers moving information between management layers. That skill is being automated. The other half, the builders, are having the best stretch of their careers. He predicts companies will cut 30,000 and hire back 8,000, all AI-first. The window to cross from "information mover" to "builder" is about two years before the new operating model locks in.

Key takeaways:

  • Judgment is the PM superpower. Testing costs dropped to near-zero. Deciding what's worth testing and whether results are good for the system is the scarce skill.
  • Build internal tools first. The highest-leverage PM work right now is automating your own org: reviews, standups, status reports. Obsolete your own process before building for customers.
  • PMs are expanding into other functions. 14 of 125 members of Singal's community became founding CEOs. PMs are being recruited into HR, marketing, and sales to replace process with software.
  • Design roles are plateauing. Design hiring isn't growing even as products multiply. PMs get more leverage from AI than designers do.
  • "In two years, there won't be any more bad software." Every terrible airline app, smart home app, and COBOL legacy system will get rebuilt. Tolerance for bad software drops to zero.

Claude Design: Wireframes and Pitch Decks Are the Real Product

Greg Isenberg Watch here

Greg stress-tests Claude Design live, building a senior brain exercises app, generating a VC pitch deck, and attempting video. His ratings: wireframing 9/10, pitch decks 8.7/10, mobile app design 8.5/10, video 4.5/10. The surprise winner was pitch deck generation. From a single idea screenshot, Claude produced market sizing (58M Americans over 65), unit economics ($62 CAC, $228 LTV, 3.3-month payback), go-to-market strategy, and speaker notes. Greg called it "the best deck I've ever seen created by any LLM. Period."

Key takeaways:

  • Start with wireframes to save tokens. Don't one-shot hi-fi designs. Wireframes clarify features and create constraints before you burn tokens on polish.
  • The clarifying questionnaire is the real product. Claude Design's pre-generation questions force you to think through device, tone, audience, and accessibility. Greg valued this more than the actual outputs.
  • Don't run multiple tasks at once. Running two projects simultaneously caused freezes and lost context. Claude Design doesn't warn you.
  • Token burn rate is a constraint. Multiple users reported burning through Claude Max tokens in 1-2 hours. Be strategic about what you request.
  • Skip video. Use Seedance 2.0 or EverSince.ai instead. Claude Design's video output is a 5/10 at best. Not worth the tokens.

Antifund: Attention as Venture Capital

20VC (Harry Stebbings) | Guests: Jake Paul, Jeffrey Woo Watch here

Jake Paul and partner Jeffrey Woo make the case for their $30M fund Antifund (portfolio includes Ramp, Cognition, Chronosphere). The thesis: in a world of abundant capital, distribution is the scarce resource. Influencer-investors provide something checks can't: awareness for portfolio companies at scale. Jeffrey argues native influencers learn VC faster than VCs build audiences. The distribution moat is harder to acquire than investing skill.

Key takeaways:

  • "VCs can't become influencers faster than influencers can learn VC." Jeffrey's thesis on why creator-led funds have a structural edge in deal-winning and portfolio support.
  • Content discipline transfers to investing. Jake's Vine-era precision (6.9 seconds, not 6, was the actual video length) carries into meticulous deal evaluation.
  • Polarizing personas are a feature. Lean into your unique strength instead of sanding down edges. Every strong founder does this.
  • Current fund is $30M. Ambition is $10-20B. Whether or not you buy the thesis, creator-led capital allocation is attracting real money.
